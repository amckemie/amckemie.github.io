What I Do

I'm a recent graduate from MakerSquare, a 12-week immersive web development bootcamp that has a project-driven, independent-learning focus. While there I worked on developing critical object-oriented and test-driven development skills. Throughout the course I worked on numerous projects (individually and with partners), allowing me to work on the following full stack web development skills: Ruby, Relational Databases (SQLite and PostegreSQL), ActiveRecord, Git, APIs, Algorithms (Sorting, Searching, Tree Traversal), Javascript, Rails, Rake, Foundation, CSS3, and HTML5.

Who Am I?

A 25-year-old lover of books, running, swimming, and dogs, I was a full-time marketing/SEO content editor before I finally gave into the unyielding urge to program. I love learning new things, meeting new people, and experiencing new things. I also enjoy giving back to the community and regularly volunteer with Big Brothers, Big Sisters and Back on My Feet.

Recent Work

At MakerSquare I worked on a variety of projects, with a focus on backend and frontend, including building my own versions of Foundation and jQuery, and designing the game of War via a LinkedList. My three main projects, however, are shown below and include:

Give A Penny, Take a Penny

a web app created by a team of 3 that aims to connect volunteers and non-profit organizations through volunteer opportunities.

Tree of Life

A web app that provides a dynamic visualization of the tree of life. I coded the backend of the project by converting data from an XML file into a relational database with over 35,000 records. I also utilized Sinatra to create an API that returns a species’ basic data, its immediate children species, and certain Wikipedia information.
